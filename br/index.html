My PhD thesis and associated materials

Scrif PhD vy ha tacklow kelmes orto

My PhD (Queen’s University Belfast, 2020) examined lexical variation in Breton and its relation to “new speaker” identity; you can read it here.

Rag an PhD vy (pednscol Béal Feirste, 2020), my a studyas an varyanjow en-mesk an geryow en Bretonek hag an kelm tredh hebma ha honanieth an “gowsoryon nowydh”. Whei a ell y redya obma.

While conducting this research I also gave a number of conference papers relating to Breton:

’Vel radn a’n whithrans na, my a wrug rei an cowsow ma ort kescussulyanjow:

  • Lexical creativity and new speaker stereotypes among users of Breton on Facebook (2020), an extended version of which was given as an invited lecture in 2021neb a wruga vy gul versyon moy hir anodho vel areth en 2021
  • Crowdsourcing dictionary entries in major and minoritised languages (2019)
  • New speakers and “néo-breton” on Facebook (2019)
  • New speakers and standards in Breton (2018)
  • Lexical variation in “néo”-Breton: A corpus-based approach (2018)
  • Standard and non-standard Breton on Facebook (2018)
  • Investigating the “new speaker” stereotype: Lexical variation in the Breton media (2018)
  • The online presence of standard and non-standard Breton (2017)
  • Facebook as a potential site for non-standard Breton (2017)
